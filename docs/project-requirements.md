**NexHack 2026**

# **1\. Introduction**

| NexHack 2026 is an AI hackathon designed to push participants, with 1 to 3 people as a team, to produce solutions that can survive beyond a one-day demo. Organised in partnership with Xenber Sdn. Bhd. as the sole sponsor and physical final host, the hackathon challenges participants to build AI systems for real enterprise operations and fintech trust problems. The competition is open to both university students and working adults in Malaysia, encouraging cross-functional teams that combine technical ability, product thinking, and business judgment. The hackathon uses an online preliminary round to identify top 10 finalist teams, followed by a one-day physical final at Xenber Sdn. Bhd. where finalists present working prototypes, live demos, technical architecture & execution, commercial reasoning, and implementation roadmaps. The strongest projects may be considered for post-hackathon development, mentorship, pilot opportunities, or funding support, subject to Xenber’s assessment and mutually agreed terms and conditions. 2\. Hackathon Philosophy: Build What the Market Will Pay For The central philosophy of NexHack 2026 is practicality. The winning solutions should not merely look impressive; they should solve painful problems that real organizations, departments, fintech companies, or customers would realistically pay to adopt. Technical depth matters: participants should demonstrate real system design, technical architecture & implementation, rational use of AI, and user experience. Business depth matters equally: participants should explain the target customers, pain point, pricing models, implementation roadmap, and commercialization potential. Depth beats breadth: judges should reward teams that solve one practical problem deeply rather than teams that build many shallow features. Builder mindset matters: the best teams should think like product builders and entrepreneurs, not only coders. 3\. Problem Statement Main theme: Building Autonomous AI Workforce & Trusted Fintech Ecosystems Organizations are under pressure to improve productivity, reduce operational risk, and respond faster to fraud, compliance, and customer threats. Participants must design and implement an AI-powered system that can assist, automate, or intelligently coordinate real business workflows while remaining explainable, controllable, and realistic for adoption. The topic chosen is less important than the depth of execution. Judges reward teams that solve one practical problem deeply rather than teams that cover many features shallowly.Participants need to propose a practical solution with their own creativity and innovation that is related to either track below. Track 1: Agentic AI for Internal Enterprise Operations (HR, Finance, Compliance, IT Operations and so on) Track 2: Fintech Risk & Fraud Intelligence (Fraud Detection, Anti-Money Laundering, Credit Risk, Scam Prevention and so on) 4\. Target Participants and Team Structure Open to the public, including students, fresh graduates, working adults, founders, engineers, designers and builders. Team size: 1 to 3 participants per team. Mixed teams are encouraged, especially teams that combine software engineering, AI/data, UX/product, domain knowledge, and business strategy. |

# **6\. Competition Format**

## **Online Preliminary Round**

- **Goal:** Select top 10 teams with strong problem understanding, clear technical direction, and realistic commercial potential.
- **Building phase:** Participants can start to build after registration & creating a team and submit anytime before the prelim submission period ends at 28 June 2026, 11:59 PM.
- **Submission:**

  8A pitch deck (attached in GitHub repository README.md) that covers the problem chosen, target users, technical architecture diagram and so on that covers both technical and business parts

1. A 7-minute demo video that covers both your app demo and presentation slides on problem statement, solution, features, technical architecture diagram, target market, pricing, implementation roadmap, both technical and business parts on YouTube (2 marks will be deducted for every 30 seconds after 7 minutes)
2. GitHub repository
3. Deployment link (optional and localhost also acceptable)

- **Judging focus:** Can this team build something meaningful, practical, technically feasible, and deep enough for the final?

## **Physical Final Round at Xenber Sdn. Bhd.**

- **Goal:** Validate working implementation, technical execution, product thinking, and business usefulness through live demonstration.
- **Format:** Opening briefing, final demo, judges Q\&A, awards.
- **Final deliverables:** Working prototype, live demo, pitch deck, technical architecture diagram and business value explanation.
