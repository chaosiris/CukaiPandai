## TL;DR — what this panel collectively rewards

This is an **engineer-heavy, commercially-minded, fintech-leaning** panel, and the sponsor is a working **software-services + AI-products** shop — not a research lab. The signal is overwhelming and consistent:

1. **Working code over slideware.** Multiple judges (JurisTech, Chin Hin, the on/off-chain engineer) explicitly value *working prototypes over decks*. A live, traceable demo beats a beautiful pitch.
2. **Agentic AI that is *deterministic, governed, explainable*** — not open-ended "magic." JurisTech literally markets "**deterministic agentic AI**" (policy-locked, fully audit-logged, human-in-the-loop). Build agents with guardrails, audit trails, and override paths.
3. **A real buyer and a price.** The sponsor is revenue-first; one judge (Macrokiosk reading) preaches "sell/validate before you scale." Name the customer, the willingness to pay, and an adoption roadmap.
4. **Malaysian fintech/enterprise relevance.** PayNet (national fraud infra), JurisTech (lending/collections), Xenber (its own **Risk & Credit AI Engine**), Chin Hin (multi-division enterprise ops). Solutions grounded in *Malaysian* regulatory and market reality (BNM, NSRC, PDPA, data sovereignty) will resonate.
5. **Responsible AI as a feature, not a footnote** — explainability, reason codes, auditability, privacy/data-sovereignty (PayNet's federated-learning bet).

**Net:** the highest-EV projects are an **explainable, governed agentic system** that targets a **named Malaysian FI/enterprise buyer**, shows a **live end-to-end demo**, and quantifies **ROI**. Track 2 (fraud/AML/credit/scam) overlaps the *most* judges' day jobs (PayNet, JurisTech, Xenber's credit engine, the on/off-chain engineer) — it is the panel's "home turf."

### Implied rubric (until the official Google Doc confirms exact weights)
Combine the brief's stated philosophy with the panel above:
1. **Problem & market pain** (real, expensive, frequent)
2. **Business depth** — named customer, pricing, adoption roadmap
3. **Technical depth & *meaningful, governed* Agentic AI** (deterministic, audit-logged, human-in-loop)
4. **Working prototype / live demo quality** (it must *run*, end to end)
5. **Responsible & explainable AI** (reason codes, auditability, privacy/data sovereignty)
6. **Presentation** (the required 7-min video carrying both tech + business)

That single description simultaneously hits Xenber (credit/fraud engine), PayNet (fraud/AML/privacy), JurisTech (governed agents in lending), and Shawn (working, secure money flows) — i.e., **4 of 5 judges at once.** Track 1 is very winnable too (Christopher Teo's exact wheelhouse), but Track 2 hits more of the room.
